The Birth of Pixar

Steve Jobs purchases the Computer Division from George
Lucas and establishes the group as an independent company,“Pixar”
At this time about 40 people are employed Pixar and Disney begin
collaboration on CAPS, the Computer Animation Production System,
which would revolutionize the creation of traditional animated films
The first check written to Pixar by a client after it's incorporation
is from Disney,for work on this project

©Pixar Animation Studios

1995

Toy Story, the world’s first computer animated feature film,
is released in theaters on November 22. It opens at #1 that weekend
and will go on to become the highest grossing film of the year,
making $192 million domestically and $362 million worldwide
Pixar’s initial public offering takes place just days later,
on November 28. It is the largest IPO of the year

©Pixar Animation Studios

20th Anniversary Of Pixar

Cars is released in theaters on June 9, accompanied by the short film
“One Man Band” The late story artist Joe Ranft is named a Disney Legend
The Walt Disney Company announces that it has agreed to purchase
Pixar Animation Studios As part of the deal,Ed Catmull and John Lasseter
also assume leadership of Walt Disney Animation Studios Pixar celebrates
its 20th anniversary

2021

Soul wins the Oscar for Best Animated Feature at the 93rd
Academy Awards Featuring characters from Soul, the short 22 vs
Earth is released on Disney+ on April 30th Directed by Enrico
Casarosa and produced by Andrea Warren, Luca is released on
Disney+ on June 18 Featuring Dug, the beloved dog and his master,
Carl, from Up, the series of shorts.Dug Days is released on Disney+ on
September 1,kicking off the annual September celebration Pixar Fest
Two new SparkShorts, Twenty Something and Nona release on Disney+ on
September 10th and 17th, respectively. The following week, a feature-length
documentary about the making of those shorts, A Spark Story, drops on
the streaming service Set in the world of Luca, Ciao Alberto premieres
on Disney+ on November 12
